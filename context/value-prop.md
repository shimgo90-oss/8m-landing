# Value Structure — 8mirrors

## Free vs paid (funnel structure)
- **Free 2-minute quiz** = the entry funnel. Not the core value. The doorway that gets people in.
- **Paid detail (the core value)** = the reason to pay:
  - Experts review over **4–5 days**
  - A **routine matched to your skin** (not generic recommendations)
  - **K-beauty direct shipping**
- The landing's job: get people **in** via the free quiz, while making the real value (matched routine, results) felt up front — laying the ground for the later paid conversion.

## The $9.99 perception problem (important)
Customers don't understand the concept/need of a "report," so they don't accept the price.
→ **Don't sell it as a "report."** Frame it as "a matched routine," "visible change in 8 weeks," "only products right for my skin" — i.e. **results & personalization**.

## Differentiators (use as trust messaging)
- **Unbiased**: not paid by brands to recommend them ("$0 paid by brands to be picked").
- **Expert + human**: not just an algorithm — real experts review.
- **Personalized**: quiz-based (the relaunch uses a 10-indicator system).

## Funnel handoff (structure)
The landing (marketing) CTA → links to the **product app's (eightmirrors) quiz entry**. The landing holds no product logic; it only gets people *in*.
